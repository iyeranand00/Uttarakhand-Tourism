About Uttarakhand

Uttarakhand (literally "Northern Portion") is a North Indian state which is the birthplace of the twin holy rivers, Ganga and Yamuna. It is oft referred to as the gateway to the Himalayas as it is a hilly state located at the foothills of the Himalayan mountains.From the holiest of temples to pristine mountainsides, bustling urban centres, swanky ski stations and rushing rivers... Uttarakhand has it all. The wide variety of tourist spots caters to pilgrims, nature lovers and adrenaline-filled adventure enthusiasts too.

Corbett National Park

India's oldest national park, 520 sq km in area, home to the Royal Bengal Tiger, contains 500 odd plant species and 800+ bird and animal species.
It is one of the most popular eco-tourism spots in Uttarakhand.

Best Time to Vist:

  1. Summer: Get a good view of all the species and enjoy rafting as well
  2. Winter: Best time to see exotic migratory birds and the Royal Bengal Tiger

Rishikesh

Rishikesh, known as the birthplace of Yoga, is a wonderful place to relax and unwind in the lap of nature.

Things to do

    • River rafting on the Ganges
    • Base for trans-Himalayan treks
    • Yoga and meditation

Best Time to visit

    • August to October

Dehradun

•Capital of Uttarakhand that is nestled in the lush green Doon valley
•Known as India's school capital as it is home to some highly reputed private boarding schools (Doon School) and the Indian Military Academy

Mussoorie



Mussoorie offers a spectacular view of the Himalayas. It is a popular weekend destination for millenials, courtesy the well-developed tourist facilities.

Best Time to visit

    • April to June

Things to do

    • Take a cable car to Gun hill
    • Enjoy a picturesque nature walk along Camel's Back Road.
    • Undertake an excursion to the Kempty Falls

Char Dham


Yamunotri, Gangotri, Kedarnath and Badrinath are holy Hindu pilgrimage sites located at the source of the 4 holy rivers Yamuna, Ganga, Mandakini and Alakananda respectively.

When to Visit

  1. Summer: April to June are the best months to visit
  2. Pre-Winter: October and November are also good times to visit if you want to beat the heat.
    • Avoid visiting during the Monsoon months(July-September) at all costs lest some unfortunate event like the 2013 floods befalls you.
    • Snowfall makes a visit during Winter hazardous and inconvenient.

Nanda Devi Biosphere Reserve


    • This entire reserve lies at an altitude greater than 3500m.
    • Numerous 6000+ metre peaks are located here, with the most well-known of them being Nanda Devi(7816m)
    • Inhabited by 300+ species of flaura and fauna with a large proportion of endemic species.

Valley of Flowers


    • High altitude valley inside the reserve that boasts of myriad vibrant hues set against mountainous snow-clad Himalayan peaks.
    • Home to endless Alpine flora and a variety of exotic, endangered animals like the snow leopard, musk deer etc.

Best Time to Visit

    • Mid-April to June
